01 / The Eduba frame

Most hardware companies automate the wrong things.

The core idea is simple. About 60% of what the team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a real AI problem. Sorting the work into those three layers before anything is built is the engagement.

Applied to a five-variant drone-dock company: field-service ticket routing is a rule-based problem. The reseller and installer knowledge base is a database and versioning problem. Compatibility matrices against DJI M300, M350, Mavic 3, and Parrot Anafi lines are structured context. Federal-capture document assembly (Blue UAS, CMMC, GSA) is where a well-instrumented AI layer actually compounds.

Sorting those four into the right layers, before anything new is built, is the work we would propose to scope with the team.

02 / The relevant case study
VigilOre Regulated industry Document-heavy workflow Multi-agent orchestration

A compliance platform for DRC mining operations. The work pattern was documentation-heavy, evidence-gated, and under scrutiny from outside auditors. Eduba built a multi-agent orchestration layer that collapsed 160-plus hours of compliance work into under five minutes of operator review per cycle.
